Learn how to get a Bitcoin loan without selling crypto. Discover how BTC-backed loans work, how investors unlock liquidity from Bitcoin, and why borrowing against crypto is becoming popular.

---

## Why Borrow Against Bitcoin?

Many Bitcoin investors believe BTC will increase in value over time.

Selling Bitcoin to access cash means losing exposure to potential price growth.

A **Bitcoin-backed loan** solves this problem.

Instead of selling BTC, investors use it as collateral to receive a loan.

---

## How Bitcoin Loans Work

Crypto-backed loans follow a simple process.

### 1. Deposit Bitcoin as Collateral

The borrower transfers Bitcoin to a secured wallet controlled by the lending platform.

The BTC acts as collateral for the loan.

---

### 2. Receive a Loan

The platform determines the loan amount using the **loan-to-value (LTV)** ratio.

Example:

- BTC collateral: **$20,000**
- LTV: **50%**

Loan amount: **$10,000**

Loans are often paid in USD or stablecoins.

---

### 3. Repay the Loan

When the borrower repays the loan and interest, the Bitcoin collateral is released back to them.

---

## Benefits of Borrowing Instead of Selling

Borrowing against Bitcoin allows investors to:

- maintain long-term BTC exposure
- access cash quickly
- avoid selling during market dips
- unlock liquidity from crypto holdings

---

## Risks to Consider

Bitcoin prices can be volatile.

If BTC drops significantly, the platform may require additional collateral to maintain the loan.

Choosing conservative LTV ratios can reduce this risk.

---

## Final Thoughts

Bitcoin loans allow investors to access liquidity without giving up ownership of their BTC.

For long-term crypto holders, borrowing against Bitcoin offers a flexible alternative to selling.
